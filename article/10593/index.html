Live At Leeds 2009 line up announced

This article was published in 2009.

Organisers have today release the line up for Live At Leeds 2009, the multi-venue festival scheduled across Leeds for Saturday 2nd May. Venues open at noon and music continues until between 10pm and 12pm depending upon the venue.

The line up is as follows.

Brudenell Social Club: Wonderswan, 1990s, Lord Auch, ffYars, These Monsters, The Mark Inside, i concur, Spondi Pradlo, Alan Pownall, Mumford And Sons, and Mi Mye.

Holy Trinity Church: Jonjo Feather, Lady And The Lost Boys, Fran Rodgers, James Yuill, Alessi's Ark, Elinor Rose Dougal, Fionn Regan, Gary Stewart, The Voluntary Butler Scheme, Paul Marshall, and Marina And The Diamonds.

Packhorse: Blacklisters, 7 Hertz, Holy State, Chops, Castrovalva, De Salvo, and Cowtown.

The Library: Year of the Man, Mt., Monster Killed By Laser, Brontide, Chickenhawk, Humanfly, and Diagonal.

The Elbow Room: Japanese Fighting Fish, Piskie Sits, Rook and the Ravens, Silverlode, Ten Bears, Kid iD, Hurricane Sunday, The Yalla Yallas, The Federals, Die Video Die, Dead Bells, James Owen Fender, Middleman, and Tommy Sparks.

Cockpit: Stations, Official Secrets Act, General Fiasco, White Light Parade, Fight Like Apes, Sky Larkin, Slow Club, iLiKETRAiNS, and Wintermute.

Cockpit Room 3: Killing Fields of Ontario, Simon Pollard, Just Handshakes (We're British), Telegraphs, Micky P Kerr, Sam Isaac, The Old Romantic Killer Band, Bicycle Thieves, Airship, and Baddies.

LMUSU: The Dharma, International Trust, Scars On 45, The Bacchae, The Brute Chorus, The ABC Club, Little Death, Bombay Bicycle Club, Dinosaur Pile-Up, Grammatics and The Maccabees.

Faversham: Sketches, Four Dead In Ohio, Barringtone, Flashguns, Stricken City, Polly Scattergood, Soft Toy Emergency, Mountain Goat Frenzy, Gold Teeth, Eskimo Twins, Dan Black and Ou Est Le Swimming Pool.

Joseph’s Well: Yonderboy, Broadcast Society, Tigers That Talked, Wild Beasts, Dartz!, The Joy Formidable, Pulled Apart By Horses, Youves, Duels, Lion Club, Club Smith, and Skint and Demoralised.

Wristbands for the event cost just £10 and allow you access to all venues throughout the day. You can buy them now from See Tickets.
