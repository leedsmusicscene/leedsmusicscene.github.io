Pulled Apart by Horses to release 'Live At Leeds' CD on April 17th

This article was published in 2010.

On Sunday, March 7th Pulled Apart by Horses played an intimate and sold-out home town show at the Packhorse, and for fans of the band you'll be happy to hear that the set was recorded.

Pulled Apart by Horses have this week announced that they have teamed up with independent label Transgressive Records to released a limited edition, vinyl only 'Live At Leeds' record on April 17th.

The release forms part of Record Store Day and is limited to only 500 copies.

In homage to The Who's classic, 'Live At Leeds' will include a set list plus an exclusive photo print taken at the gig. There are five different photos available to collect.

The set featured tracks such as 'E=MC Hammer' and 'I Punched A Lion In The Throat' along side one of the band's newer songs 'Yeah Buddy', which will be included on Pulled Apart by Horses' debut album.

The record is vinyl only but you'll be able to download sample tracks for free from Friday 16th April from the Transgressive site.

To celebrate Record Store Day and the live release Pulled Apart by Horses will play live in-store at Crash Records in Leeds at 6:30 pm on 17th April.
