Pulled Apart by Horses added to Leeds Festival line up

This article was published in 2014.

Organisers of the annual Leeds Festival have announced over 50 new bands for the festival to be held this coming August Bank Holiday weekend.

One of those new additions was Leeds boys Pulled Apart by Horses, who will take an early afternoon Main Stage slot on the Sunday.

Completing the line-up for the Main Stage are Blood Red Shoes, Crossfaith, Dry the River, Gnarwolves, Papa Roach, Young Guns, Tonight Alive and The Story So Far.

They will be joining 2014 headliners Arctic Monkeys, Blink 182, and co-headliners Paramore and Queens Of The Stone Age.

Alongside headliners Courteeners, Bombay Bicycle Club and Disclosure on the NME / Radio One Stage will be Twin Atlantic, Mallory Knox, Lonely The Brave, The Horrors, The Kooks and Twin Shadow.

Headliners for the Lock Up Stage were confirmed this week as being Gogol Bordello (Friday), Jimmy Eat World (Saturday) and Of Mice & Men (Sunday).

Another stage also revealed its headliners yesterday, with Band Of Skulls, Palma Violets and Augustines taking the top honours on the Festival Republic Stage.

If that wasn't enough, the organisers also confirmed another list of additions for Leeds 2014: A Wilhelm Scream, Baby Godzilla, Bear Hands, Bipolar Sunshine, Catfish And The Bottlemen, Darlia, Dave Hause, Every Time I Die, Hudson Taylor, Marika Hackman, Mayday Parade, Meridian Dan, P Money, PUP, Slaves, The Districts, The Flatliners, The Front Bottoms, The Orwells, The Skints, The Wonder Years, Touché Amoré and Wovenwar.

Leeds Festival takes place between the 22nd-24th August at Bramham Park. Weekend Tickets are on sale now priced at £202.50 and Day Tickets are available at £92.50.
