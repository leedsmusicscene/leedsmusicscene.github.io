The Scaramanga Six will release a new album on 20th April

This article was published in 2009.

Huddersfield band The Scaramanga Six will release their brand new studio album later this month. Titled ‘Songs Of Prey’ the mammoth 14-track, hour-long album is released from 20th April 2009 and is available to pre-order now.

The Scaramanga Six are a self-professed ‘evil pop group’, lurking and ready to pounce on the ears of the unsuspecting. They are led by the infamous Morricone Brothers, Steven and Paul, and have been a major part of the UK music scene for well over a decade.

‘Songs of Prey’ is the band’s fifth studio album release and was recorded and produced by James Kenosha [Duels, Grammatics, This Et Al and Pulled Apart By Horses]. The album comes presented in rough stock Digipack format with a 16-page booklet bursting with lyrics, imagery and photos.

The album tracklisting is as follows: 1. Hole In My Emotion; 2. Self Destruct; 3. I Didn't Get Where I Am Today; 4. You Should Have Killed Me When You Had The Chance; 5. Song of Prey #1; 6. Misadventure; 7. Another Coward; 8. Groom of The Stool; 9. Song of Prey #2; 10. Back to School; 11. Sophia In Blue; 12. The Twentieth of Hell; 13. By-Product; and 14. Pink & Blue.

The Scaramanga Six celebrate the release of ‘Songs Of Prey’ with an album launch part at Joseph’s Well on Saturday 25th April. Support comes from The FF’ers and Cyanide Pills.
